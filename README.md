# ImperialPredict
ImperialPredict is an advanced grade calculator and degree planner for Imperial College students. It lets you input marks for coursework and exams to calculate your weighted module scores, predict your final degree, and strategize for future assignments.

This is a personal project aimed at simplifying the grade tracking process, replacing manual spreadsheets with a dedicated, interactive tool.

**Live Application:** https://sohan-suchdev.github.io/Imperial_Predict/

## Features

- **Real-Time Calculation:** Instantly updates module and year averages as you input grades or adjust sliders.
- **Weighted Scoring:** Handles complex module weightings and credit values automatically behind the scenes.
- **Degree Classification:** Displays your current predicted degree class (1st, 2:1, 2:2) based on official mark bands.
- **Auto-Save:** Your inputs are automatically saved to your browser's local storage. You will never lose your progress if you close the tab.
- **Smart Exclusions:** Uncheck specific assignments or whole modules that you haven't completed yet to get an accurate "current" average based only on submitted work.
- **🛡️ Pass Mode (40%):** A "worst-case scenario" toggle that temporarily treats all your uncompleted assignments as a bare pass (40%), showing you the absolute floor of your final grade.
- **🎯 Target Predictor & Auto-Fill:** - Select your target degree (e.g., 2:1 or 60%), and the app will tell you the exact average you need on your remaining work. 
  - Click **Auto-Fill** to instantly set your uncompleted assignments to the required marks.
  - **Interactive See-Saw:** Adjust any auto-filled slider, and the app will automatically balance the other sliders to ensure you still hit your exact target grade!
- **📊 Module Insights & Term Trajectory:** - See how your genuine term averages are trending across the year with a visual chart.
  - Click the **ℹ️ Insights** button on any supported module to view a chart of historical grade distributions and read brief, actionable examiner feedback on common mistakes.

## Supported Courses

Currently, the application supports the following courses (updated for the 2025/26 academic year):

- Computing (BEng/MEng) - Year 1 *(Fully supported with Insights)*
- Electrical & Electronic Engineering (EEE) - Year 1
- Civil Engineering - Year 1
- Design Engineering - Year 1
